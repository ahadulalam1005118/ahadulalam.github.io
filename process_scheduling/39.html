Scheduling Details
•
Result approximates SRTF
–
CPU bound jobs drop rapidly to lower queues
–
Short-running I/O bound jobs stay near the top
•
Scheduling must be done between the queues
–
Fixed priority scheduling: serve all from the highest priority, then the next
priority, etc.
–
Time slice: each queue gets a certain amount of CPU time (e.g., 70% to the
highest, 20% next, 10% lowest)
•
Countermeasure: user action that can foil intent of the OS designer
–
For multilevel feedback, put in a bunch of meaningless I/O to keep job’s
priority high
–
But if everyone does this, it won’t work!
–
Consider an Othello program, playing against a competitor.
Key was to
compute at a higher priority than the competitors.
•
Put in printf’s, run much faster!
